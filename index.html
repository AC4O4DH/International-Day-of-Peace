What is the International Day of Peace?

The International Day of Peace, also officially known as World Peace Day, is a United Nations-sanctioned holiday observed annually on September 21st.

It is dedicated to world peace, and specifically the absence of war and violence. The day is marked by activities such as moments of silence, educational events, and calls for ceasefires in conflict zones around the world.

Core Purpose: To strengthen the ideals of peace, both within and among all nations and peoples, and to provide a globally shared date for all humanity to commit to peace above all differences.

A Brief History

1981
The United Nations General Assembly established the International Day of Peace, initially celebrated on the third Tuesday of September.
2001
The UN General Assembly unanimously voted to designate September 21st as a permanent date for the observance, making it easier for people worldwide to participate together.
Today
The day is observed by many nations, political groups, military groups, and people around the world. Activities include educational programs, art exhibitions, peace concerts, and moments of silence at noon.

Theme for 2025

This theme emphasizes the importance of building peaceful societies through education, dialogue, and understanding. It calls on individuals, communities, and nations to actively create environments where peace can flourish through mutual respect, tolerance, and cooperation.
